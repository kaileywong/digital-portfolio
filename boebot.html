Boe–Bot is short for Board of Education robot, the name of a robot kit that is used in education. This kit consists of a main circuit board (the Board of Education) and breadboard, a plug–in BASIC Stamp micro-controller, two servo motors to drive the wheels, and an aluminum chassis that the parts bolt onto.

Turning




The Boe-Bot travels in a straight line, makes a 90 degree turn, and then continues straight.






Circle



The Boe-Bot travels 360 degrees in a circle.










Letters



The Boe-Bot travels and makes two letters of the alphabet: L and J.










Wave



The Boe-Bot travels in a wave path, completing 3 cycles.










Three-Point Turn



The Boe-Bot travels towards an obstacle, backs up, does a 3 point turn, and then returns to it’s starting point.










Using Whiskers



Using it’s feelers, the Boe-Bot travels until it collides with an object and then makes a right turn.










Escape the Box



Using it's feelers, the Boe-Bot travels until it runs into the side of a box and "feels” its way out of the trap by figuring out which direction has the door.










Around the Desks



The Boe-Bot travels in a square around a group of desks.










Shapes



The Boe-Bot travels, making a square, circle, triangle, and oval, pausing 5 seconds in between each shape.
